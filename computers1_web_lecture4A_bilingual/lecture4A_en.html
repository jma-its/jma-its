Part 1 — Why Statics Matters

Statics helps us check safety and balance in hospital equipment.

Slide 1

Statics for Medical Equipment

Forces. Balance. Supports. Moments.

Today we start the statics part of mechanics. Statics means the object is not moving, but forces are still working.

A bed, chair, table, lift, or wheelchair must be safe and balanced.

Today we learn how to see forces and calculate simple balance.

Think and answer

What does statics study?

Show answer

Objects at rest, with balanced forces.

Slide 2

Why Do We Need Statics?

Hospital equipment must be safe.

A bed must not break. A wheelchair must not fall. An operating table must stay stable.

Statics helps us check balance and safety.

This is important for medical IT support because you may work near real devices, not only computers.

Think and answer

Name one hospital object that must be balanced.

Show answer

Bed, wheelchair, patient lift, operating table, cart.

Slide 3

From Materials to Mechanics

A strong material is not enough.

Last lesson was about materials. Now we study forces on those materials.

Even strong metal can fail if force is too large or placed badly.

So we ask: what force acts here? Where does it act? Can the object stay balanced?

Think and answer

Why are materials alone not enough?

Show answer

Because shape, force, distance, and balance also matter.

Part 2 — Mass, Weight, Force, and Equilibrium

We learn the basic words and formulas before solving tasks.

Slide 4

What Is Statics?

Statics = no movement.

The object is not moving. But this does not mean there is no force.

Forces can push down, supports can push up, and the object can still stay still.

Statics studies these balanced forces.

Think and answer

Can an object at rest have forces on it?

Show answer

Yes. The forces can be balanced.

Slide 5

Mass and Weight

Mass is kg. Weight is force.

Mass is the amount of matter. We measure mass in kilograms.

Weight is the force from gravity. We measure weight in newtons.

Formula: W = m g.

Think and answer

Is kg a unit of force?

Show answer

No. kg is mass. Newton is force.

Slide 6

Example: Patient Weight

Use g = 10 m/s².

A patient has mass 70 kg.

Weight is W = m g.

W = 70 × 10 = 700 N.

So the patient pushes down with 700 newtons.

Think and answer

What is the weight of a 70 kg patient if g = 10?

Show answer

700 N.

Slide 7

What Is Force?

A force can push, pull, or press down.

Force can push a bed away from a person.

Force can pull a cart toward a person.

Weight is also a force. It acts downward because of gravity.

Force has direction.

Think and answer

Give one example of force.

Show answer

Push, pull, lift, press, or weight.

Slide 8

Force Arrows

We draw forces with arrows.

An arrow shows direction.

An arrow also shows where the force acts.

A longer arrow can show a bigger force.

In statics diagrams, arrows are very important.

Think and answer

What does a force arrow show?

Show answer

Direction, place, and sometimes size.

Slide 9

Free-Body Diagram

Simplify the real object.

A free-body diagram removes extra details.

We keep only the object and the forces.

This helps us calculate and not get confused by the picture.

Think and answer

Why do we use a free-body diagram?

Show answer

To focus only on the forces.

Slide 10

Equilibrium: No Vertical Movement

Upward forces equal downward forces.

If the bed is not moving up or down, vertical forces are balanced.

The condition is: sum of vertical forces equals zero.

In simple words: support forces up equal weight down.

Think and answer

What does equilibrium mean?

Show answer

Balance. No acceleration.

Slide 11

Patient in the Middle

Two supports share the weight equally.

The patient is in the middle of the bed.

The left and right supports are symmetric.

So the reactions are equal: RA = RB.

If W = 700 N, each support gets 350 N.

Think and answer

Why are the support forces equal here?

Show answer

Because the patient is in the middle.

Slide 12

Why Half and Half?

Use two simple equations.

The total upward force must equal the downward weight.

RA + RB = W.

If the patient is in the middle, RA = RB.

So each support carries W divided by 2.

Think and answer

If W = 600 N in the middle, what is each support?

Show answer

300 N and 300 N.

Slide 13

Support Reactions

Supports push up.

The patient pushes down on the bed.

The bed pushes down on the supports.

The supports push up on the bed.

These upward forces are called support reactions: RA and RB.

Think and answer

What direction do support reactions act?

Show answer

Upward.

Part 3 — Moments and Support Reactions

Off-center loads need moments. The closer support gets bigger force.

Slide 14

Patient Is Not in the Middle

The closer support gets bigger force.

Now the patient is closer to one support.

The two reactions are no longer equal.

The closer support carries more load.

To calculate this, we need moments.

Think and answer

Are the supports always equal?

Show answer

No. They are equal only in symmetric cases.

Slide 15

Moment of Force

Moment is turning effect.

A force can make an object rotate.

This turning effect is called moment.

Formula: M = F × d.

F is force. d is distance from the pivot.

Think and answer

What makes moment bigger?

Show answer

Bigger force or bigger distance from the pivot.

Slide 16

Door Example

Distance matters.

Try opening a door near the hinge. It is hard.

Now push far from the hinge. It is easier.

The force can be the same, but the distance is different.

That is why the moment is different.

Think and answer

Where is it easier to push a door?

Show answer

Far from the hinge.

Slide 17

Seesaw Rule

Left moment equals right moment.

A seesaw is a simple example of moments.

One person creates a moment on the left.

The other creates a moment on the right.

For balance: W1 × d1 = W2 × d2.

Think and answer

What is the balance rule for a seesaw?

Show answer

Left moment = right moment.

Slide 18

Heavy and Light Person

Heavy person closer. Light person farther.

If one person is heavier, they must sit closer to the center.

If one person is lighter, they must sit farther away.

This makes the moments equal.

This is the same idea used in bed support calculations.

Think and answer

Where should the heavier person sit?

Show answer

Closer to the fulcrum.

Slide 19

Two Conditions for Statics

No movement and no rotation.

For full static equilibrium, we need two conditions.

First: sum of vertical forces is zero.

Second: sum of moments is zero.

In symbols: ΣFy = 0 and ΣM = 0.

Think and answer

What are the two statics conditions?

Show answer

Sum of forces equals zero, and sum of moments equals zero.

Slide 20

Take Moment About Point A

Choose a pivot to calculate.

We can take moments about point A.

A force at point A has zero distance from A, so it makes no moment about A.

This helps us remove RA from the moment equation.

Then we can find RB first.

Think and answer

Why take moments about point A?

Show answer

Because RA has zero moment about A.

Slide 21

Support Force Formulas

For one point load between two supports.

If the load W is between two supports, use distances a and b.

RA = W × b / (a + b).

RB = W × a / (a + b).

The closer support gets the bigger reaction.

Think and answer

If the patient is closer to A, which reaction is bigger?

Show answer

RA is bigger.

Slide 22

Example Task: Patient Off-Center

Given W, a, and b.

Patient weight is W = 700 N.

Distance from left support to patient is a = 1 m.

Distance from patient to right support is b = 2 m.

Find RA and RB.

Think and answer

What is the total distance between supports?

Show answer

a + b = 3 m.

Slide 23

Step 1: Weight and Total Force

Start with vertical force balance.

The total downward force is W = 700 N.

The total upward force is RA + RB.

Because the bed is balanced: RA + RB = 700 N.

This is our force check.

Think and answer

What must RA + RB equal?

Show answer

700 N.

Slide 24

Step 2: Moment Balance

Use the moment formula.

RA = W × b / (a + b).

RA = 700 × 2 / 3 = 467 N.

RB = W × a / (a + b).

RB = 700 × 1 / 3 = 233 N.

Check: 467 + 233 = 700 N.

Think and answer

Which support force is bigger?

Show answer

RA, because the patient is closer to A.

Slide 25

Formulas for One Point Load

Use only for this simple case.

These formulas are useful for one point load between two supports.

RA = W × b / (a + b).

RB = W × a / (a + b).

Do not use them blindly for every problem.

For more complex tasks, write force balance and moment balance.

Think and answer

Why should we not use formulas blindly?

Show answer

Because different problems can have different force arrangements.

Part 4 — Practice Tasks and Summary

Use the same ideas again and again: W = mg, ΣF = 0, M = F × d, ΣM = 0.

Slide 26

Practice Task 1: Centered Patient

Try a simple symmetric case.

A patient has mass 60 kg.

Use g = 10 m/s².

The patient is in the middle.

Find W, RA, and RB.

Think and answer

Answer?

Show answer

W = 600 N. RA = 300 N. RB = 300 N.

Slide 27

Practice Task 2: Off-Center Patient

Use the formulas.

Given W = 800 N.

a = 1 m.

b = 3 m.

Find RA and RB.

Think and answer

Answer?

Show answer

RA = 800 × 3 / 4 = 600 N. RB = 800 × 1 / 4 = 200 N.

Slide 28

Practice Task 3: Seesaw

Use moment balance.

Person 1: W1 = 600 N and d1 = 2 m.

Person 2: W2 = 400 N.

Find d2.

Balance rule: W1 d1 = W2 d2.

Think and answer

Answer?

Show answer

d2 = 600 × 2 / 400 = 3 m.

Slide 29

Practice Task 4: Bed + Equipment

Two loads on one bed.

A patient pushes down with 500 N.

A machine pushes down with 300 N.

Use the distances on the diagram.

Find RA and RB.

Think and answer

What is the first step?

Show answer

Add vertical forces: RA + RB = 800 N.

Slide 30

Practice Task 5: Will It Tip?

Compare moments around the pivot.

The bed and patient create moments around the pivot.

To know if it tips, compare clockwise and counterclockwise moments.

If the tipping moment is bigger than the stabilizing moment, the bed can tip.

This is a safety question.

Think and answer

What should we compare?

Show answer

Moments around the pivot.

Slide 31

Common Mistakes

Avoid these mistakes.

Do not use kg as force.

Do not forget g.

Do not ignore direction.

Do not forget moment.

Do not think supports are always equal.

Think and answer

When are support forces equal?

Show answer

Only in symmetric cases, such as load in the middle.

Slide 32

Summary

Statics helps us check balance and safety.

Weight: W = m g.

Equilibrium: ΣF = 0.

Moment: M = F × d.

No rotation: ΣM = 0.

Support forces can be different when the load is not in the middle.

Think and answer

What is the most important safety idea?

Show answer

Check force balance and moment balance.
