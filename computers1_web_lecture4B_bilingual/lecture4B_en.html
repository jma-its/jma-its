Part 1 — What Is Kinematics?

Kinematics describes motion. We do not study forces yet.

Slide 1

Kinematics for Medical Equipment

Position. Speed. Velocity. Acceleration.

Today we study kinematics. Kinematics means motion.

We describe how objects move. We do not ask why they move yet.

Forces will come in the next lecture: dynamics.

Today we use simple formulas and many small tasks.

Think and answer

What does kinematics study?

Show answer

Motion. It describes how objects move.

Slide 2

From Statics to Kinematics

Statics: no movement. Kinematics: movement.

Last time we studied statics. The object did not move.

Today objects can move: a bed, a wheelchair, a lift, a cart, or a scanner table.

Statics asks: Is it balanced?

Kinematics asks: How does it move?

Think and answer

What is the main difference between statics and kinematics?

Show answer

Statics studies objects at rest. Kinematics studies motion.

Slide 3

Why Kinematics Matters in Hospitals

Many hospital machines move.

A wheelchair moves along the floor.

A hospital bed can move forward, backward, up, and down.

A patient lift moves a patient slowly and safely.

A scanner table moves a patient into a machine.

We need simple words to describe this motion.

Think and answer

Name one hospital machine that moves.

Show answer

Wheelchair, bed, patient lift, scanner table, cart, centrifuge.

Part 2 — Position, Distance, and Displacement

First we describe where something is and how far it moves.

Slide 4

Position

Position = where something is.

Position tells us where an object is.

We can describe position with a number.

Example: a bed is 2 meters from the wall.

If the bed moves, the position changes.

Think and answer

What does position mean?

Show answer

Where something is.

Slide 5

Distance

Distance = how much path.

Distance means the total path traveled.

Distance is always positive.

If a wheelchair moves 5 meters, the distance is 5 meters.

Distance does not tell direction.

Think and answer

Can distance be negative?

Show answer

No. Distance is always positive.

Slide 6

Displacement

Displacement = change in position.

Displacement means start position to finish position.

It has direction.

Formula: Δx = x₂ − x₁.

Displacement can be positive, negative, or zero.

Think and answer

What formula do we use for displacement?

Show answer

Δx = x₂ − x₁.

Slide 7

Distance vs Displacement

Distance is total path. Displacement is start to finish.

Distance and displacement can be different.

Example: move forward 3 m, then back 1 m.

Total distance is 4 m.

Displacement is 2 m forward.

Think and answer

If you move forward 3 m and back 1 m, what is the distance?

Show answer

4 m.

Part 3 — Speed, Velocity, and Acceleration

We learn the main formulas for motion.

Slide 8

Speed

Speed = distance / time.

Speed tells us how fast something moves.

Formula: v = s / t.

Unit: m/s.

If distance is large and time is small, speed is high.

Think and answer

What is the formula for speed?

Show answer

v = s / t.

Slide 9

Speed Example

A wheelchair moves 12 m in 6 s.

We calculate speed using v = s / t.

Distance is 12 meters.

Time is 6 seconds.

v = 12 / 6 = 2 m/s.

Think and answer

What is the speed?

Show answer

2 m/s.

Slide 10

Velocity

Velocity = speed + direction.

Speed only tells how fast.

Velocity tells how fast and in what direction.

Example: speed is 2 m/s.

Velocity is 2 m/s forward.

Velocity can be negative if the object moves in the opposite direction.

Think and answer

What does velocity include that speed does not?

Show answer

Direction.

Slide 11

Speed vs Velocity

Speed has no direction. Velocity has direction.

Speed is a scalar.

Velocity is a vector.

For this class, remember the simple idea:

Speed = how fast.

Velocity = how fast and where.

Think and answer

Is velocity a vector?

Show answer

Yes. It has direction.

Slide 12

What Is Acceleration?

Acceleration = change of velocity.

Acceleration means velocity changes.

The object can speed up.

The object can slow down.

The object can change direction.

All of these are acceleration.

Think and answer

Can slowing down be acceleration?

Show answer

Yes. It is negative acceleration.

Slide 13

Acceleration Formula

Acceleration = change in velocity / time.

Formula: a = Δv / Δt.

Also: a = (v₂ − v₁) / t.

Unit: m/s².

If velocity changes a lot in a short time, acceleration is large.

Think and answer

What is the unit of acceleration?

Show answer

m/s².

Slide 14

Acceleration Example

A bed changes speed.

A bed starts from rest: v₁ = 0 m/s.

Later the bed moves at v₂ = 2 m/s.

Time is t = 4 s.

a = (2 − 0) / 4 = 0.5 m/s².

Think and answer

What is the acceleration?

Show answer

0.5 m/s².

Slide 15

Positive and Negative Acceleration

Speed up is positive. Slow down is negative.

If speed increases in the positive direction, acceleration is positive.

If speed decreases in the positive direction, acceleration is negative.

In hospitals, sudden starts and stops can be dangerous.

Gentle stopping is safer for patients.

Think and answer

Why should hospital equipment stop gently?

Show answer

Because sudden motion can be unsafe for patients.

Part 4 — Graphs and Derivatives

Graphs and derivatives show change with time.

Slide 16

Position–Time Graph

Steeper line = faster motion.

A position-time graph shows where an object is at each time.

If the line is flat, the object does not move.

If the line is steep, the object moves faster.

The slope shows velocity.

Think and answer

What does a flat position-time graph mean?

Show answer

No movement.

Slide 17

Speed–Time Graph

Rising line = acceleration.

A speed-time graph shows speed at each time.

A flat line means constant speed.

A rising line means speeding up.

A falling line means slowing down.

Think and answer

What does a rising speed-time graph mean?

Show answer

Acceleration / speeding up.

Slide 18

Why Do We Need Derivatives?

A derivative shows change.

A derivative means rate of change.

How fast does position change? That gives velocity.

How fast does velocity change? That gives acceleration.

This is the main idea. Do not be afraid of the notation.

Think and answer

What does a derivative show?

Show answer

How fast something changes.

Slide 19

Derivative Idea: Small Steps

Look at very small time.

Take a very small time interval Δt.

During this time, position changes by Δx.

Velocity is approximately Δx / Δt.

When Δt becomes very small, this idea becomes a derivative.

Think and answer

What does Δx / Δt mean?

Show answer

Change in position divided by change in time.

Slide 20

Derivative Notation

v = dx/dt and a = dv/dt.

dx means a very small change in position.

dt means a very small change in time.

v = dx/dt means velocity is change of position per time.

a = dv/dt means acceleration is change of velocity per time.

Think and answer

What does dx/dt mean in this lesson?

Show answer

Velocity.

Slide 21

Position → Velocity → Acceleration

x → v → a.

This is the most important chain.

Position changes with time, and we get velocity.

Velocity changes with time, and we get acceleration.

Motion means change with time.

Think and answer

What do we get when position changes with time?

Show answer

Velocity.

Part 5 — Constant Speed and Motion from Rest

Now we use formulas for simple motion.

Slide 22

Constant Speed

Constant speed means speed does not change.

At constant speed, the object moves the same distance every second.

Formula: s = v × t.

This is useful for simple cart and wheelchair movement.

Think and answer

What is the formula for distance at constant speed?

Show answer

s = v × t.

Slide 23

Constant Speed Example

A cart moves at 1.5 m/s for 8 s.

The speed is constant.

Use s = v × t.

s = 1.5 × 8.

s = 12 m.

Think and answer

How far does the cart move?

Show answer

12 m.

Slide 24

Motion from Rest

If object starts from rest, v = a × t.

Starts from rest means the first velocity is zero.

If acceleration is constant, final velocity is v = a × t.

Distance can be calculated by s = 1/2 a t².

This is useful for lifts and moving tables.

Think and answer

What does 'starts from rest' mean?

Show answer

Initial velocity is zero.

Slide 25

Motion from Rest Example

A lift starts from rest.

A patient lift starts from rest.

Acceleration is a = 0.5 m/s².

Time is t = 4 s.

Use v = a × t.

v = 0.5 × 4 = 2 m/s.

Think and answer

What is the final velocity?

Show answer

2 m/s.

Part 6 — Practice Tasks and Summary

Solve small tasks. Check units carefully.

Slide 26

Task 1: Speed

A wheelchair moves 10 m in 5 s.

This is the simplest speed task.

Use v = s / t.

Distance is 10 m.

Time is 5 s.

v = 10 / 5 = 2 m/s.

Think and answer

Find the speed.

Show answer

2 m/s.

Slide 27

Task 2: Time

A cart moves 18 m at 3 m/s.

Now we find time.

Use t = s / v.

Distance is 18 m.

Speed is 3 m/s.

t = 18 / 3 = 6 s.

Think and answer

Find the time.

Show answer

6 s.

Slide 28

Task 3: Distance

A scanner table moves at 0.4 m/s for 10 s.

Use s = v × t.

Speed is 0.4 m/s.

Time is 10 s.

s = 0.4 × 10 = 4 m.

Think and answer

Find the distance.

Show answer

4 m.

Slide 29

Task 4: Acceleration

A bed changes speed from 0 to 1.2 m/s in 3 s.

Use a = (v₂ − v₁) / t.

v₁ = 0 m/s.

v₂ = 1.2 m/s.

t = 3 s.

a = 1.2 / 3 = 0.4 m/s².

Think and answer

Find the acceleration.

Show answer

0.4 m/s².

Slide 30

Task 5: Slowing Down

A cart slows from 2 m/s to 0 m/s in 4 s.

Use a = (v₂ − v₁) / t.

v₁ = 2 m/s.

v₂ = 0 m/s.

t = 4 s.

a = (0 − 2) / 4 = −0.5 m/s².

Negative means slowing down.

Think and answer

Find the acceleration.

Show answer

−0.5 m/s².

Slide 31

Task 6: From Rest

A lift starts from rest.

Acceleration is a = 0.3 m/s².

Time is t = 5 s.

Use v = a × t.

v = 0.3 × 5 = 1.5 m/s.

Think and answer

Find the final velocity.

Show answer

1.5 m/s.

Slide 32

Task 7: Mixed Task

A wheelchair moves in two parts.

First: 6 m in 3 s.

Then: 8 m in 4 s.

Total distance is 14 m.

Total time is 7 s.

Average speed = 14 / 7 = 2 m/s.

Think and answer

Find the average speed.

Show answer

2 m/s.

Slide 33

Common Mistakes

Do not confuse the basic words and units.

Do not confuse distance and displacement.

Do not confuse speed and velocity.

Do not confuse speed and acceleration.

Do not confuse m/s and m/s².

These mistakes are common, so check units carefully.

Think and answer

What is the unit of speed?

Show answer

m/s.

Slide 34

Summary

Motion = change with time.

Today we learned position, distance, displacement, speed, velocity, acceleration, and derivatives.

Velocity is change of position with time.

Acceleration is change of velocity with time.

Next: dynamics. Dynamics asks why motion changes.

Think and answer

What is the next mechanics topic?

Show answer

Dynamics.
