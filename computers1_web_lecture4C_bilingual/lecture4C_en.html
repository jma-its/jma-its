Part 1 — From Kinematics to Dynamics

Dynamics explains why motion changes: forces cause acceleration.

Slide 1

Dynamics for Medical Equipment

Forces. Motion. Newton’s Laws. Energy.

Today we study dynamics. Dynamics connects force and motion.

In kinematics, we described motion. We asked: how does it move?

Now we ask a deeper question: why does motion change?

The answer is force. Force can create acceleration.

Think and answer

What does dynamics study?

Show answer

Force and motion. Dynamics explains why motion changes.

Slide 2

Three Parts of Mechanics

Statics, kinematics, dynamics.

Mechanics can be divided into three parts.

Statics: the object does not move. Forces are balanced.

Kinematics: the object moves, and we describe the motion.

Dynamics: forces cause motion to change.

This lecture is the bridge from formulas to real machines.

Think and answer

Which part studies forces that change motion?

Show answer

Dynamics.

Slide 3

Why Dynamics Matters in Hospitals

Safe equipment must move smoothly.

Many hospital devices move: beds, wheelchairs, carts, lifts, scanner tables, and centrifuges.

If the motion is sudden, a patient can be frightened or injured.

If the force is too large, equipment can be damaged.

Dynamics helps us think about safe, controlled movement.

Think and answer

Name one hospital device where smooth motion is important.

Show answer

Patient lift, bed, wheelchair, scanner table, cart.

Slide 4

Force Changes Motion

Force can start, stop, speed up, slow down, or change direction.

A force is not only a push or pull. A force can change motion.

It can start motion from rest.

It can stop a moving object.

It can make motion faster or slower.

It can also change direction.

Think and answer

What are two things a force can do to motion?

Show answer

Start, stop, speed up, slow down, or change direction.

Slide 5

Inertia

Inertia = resistance to change of motion.

Inertia means an object tends to keep its current state of motion.

If it is at rest, it tends to stay at rest.

If it is moving, it tends to keep moving.

A heavy hospital bed has more inertia than a small cart.

Think and answer

Which has more inertia: a heavy bed or a light cart?

Show answer

A heavy bed.

Slide 6

Heavy Bed vs Light Cart

Same push, different acceleration.

Imagine the same push acts on a light cart and a heavy bed.

The light cart accelerates more.

The heavy bed accelerates less.

The force is the same, but the mass is different.

This idea leads to Newton’s second law.

Think and answer

Why does the heavy bed accelerate less?

Show answer

Because it has more mass / more inertia.

Part 2 — Newton’s Laws and Free-Body Diagrams

Newton’s laws help us calculate and understand motion safely.

Slide 7

Newton’s First Law

If total force is zero, motion does not change.

Newton’s first law is the law of inertia.

If the net force is zero, there is no acceleration.

An object at rest stays at rest.

An object moving keeps moving with constant velocity.

Formula idea: ΣF = 0 → a = 0.

Think and answer

If net force is zero, what is acceleration?

Show answer

Zero.

Slide 8

Newton’s Second Law

Force causes acceleration.

Newton’s second law is the main formula of dynamics.

F = m × a.

If force increases, acceleration increases.

If mass increases, acceleration decreases.

We can also write a = F / m.

Think and answer

What is Newton’s second law formula?

Show answer

F = m × a.

Slide 9

Meaning of F = ma

Force, mass, acceleration, and units.

F means force. Unit: newton, N.

m means mass. Unit: kilogram, kg.

a means acceleration. Unit: m/s².

One newton is the force needed to give 1 kg an acceleration of 1 m/s².

Think and answer

What is the unit of force?

Show answer

Newton, N.

Slide 10

Newton’s Third Law

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Forces come in pairs.

If a patient presses a bed down, the bed presses the patient up.

The two forces are equal in size and opposite in direction.

But they act on different objects, so they do not cancel each other on one object.

Think and answer

Do action and reaction act on the same object?

Show answer

No. They act on different objects.

Slide 11

Free-Body Diagram Review

Draw the object and the forces only.

A free-body diagram helps us think clearly.

Do not draw every detail of the real object.

Draw the object as a simple shape.

Draw force arrows: gravity, normal force, push, friction, tension, and so on.

Think and answer

Why do we use a free-body diagram?

Show answer

To focus only on forces.

Slide 12

Forces on a Moving Cart

Push, friction, gravity, and normal force.

A moving cart has several forces.

Gravity pulls down.

The floor pushes up with normal force.

A person pushes forward.

Friction acts backward.

Horizontal motion depends mainly on push force minus friction.

Think and answer

Which force usually acts opposite to motion?

Show answer

Friction.

Slide 13

Net Force

Net force = total force.

Net force means the final total force after adding forces with directions.

If forces act in opposite directions, we subtract.

Example: F_net = F_push − F_friction.

Acceleration is in the direction of the net force.

Think and answer

If push is 80 N and friction is 20 N, what is net force?

Show answer

60 N forward.

Part 3 — Gravity, Weight, and Apparent Weight

Gravity and weight are related, but they are not exactly the same idea.

Slide 14

Gravity Pulls Down

Gravitational force is Fg = mg.

Gravity is the force from Earth.

Earth pulls objects downward.

For a patient, the gravitational force is Fg = mg.

The direction is downward, toward Earth.

Think and answer

What is the formula for gravitational force?

Show answer

Fg = mg.

Slide 15

What Is Weight?

In physics, weight is force on a support or suspension.

In everyday language, people often say weight when they mean body mass.

In physics, weight has a more exact meaning.

Weight is the force with which a body presses on a support or pulls on a suspension.

A patient on a bed presses the bed. That pressing force is weight.

Think and answer

In physics, what is weight?

Show answer

Force on a support or suspension.

Slide 16

At Rest: Same Size

Fg and weight can have the same size at rest.

A patient rests on a bed.

Earth pulls the patient down: Fg = mg.

The patient presses the bed: W = mg.

In this simple case, the sizes are the same.

But the meanings are different.

Think and answer

Are gravitational force and weight always the same idea?

Show answer

No. They can have the same size, but the meanings are different.

Slide 17

Important Difference

Gravity pulls the body. Weight acts on support or suspension.

Gravitational force is Earth pulling the body down.

Weight is the force on the support or suspension.

These can be different when the support accelerates.

This is why you can feel heavier or lighter in an elevator.

Think and answer

When can weight change?

Show answer

When the support or suspension accelerates.

Slide 18

Elevator Going Up

You feel heavier.

If an elevator accelerates upward, the floor must push harder on the person.

The person feels heavier.

The gravitational force is still mg.

But the apparent weight is larger: W_app = m(g + a).

Think and answer

Does gravity increase when the elevator goes up?

Show answer

No. Apparent weight increases, but gravity is still mg.

Slide 19

Elevator Going Down

You feel lighter.

If an elevator accelerates downward, the floor pushes less strongly.

The person feels lighter.

The gravitational force is still mg.

The apparent weight is smaller: W_app = m(g − a).

Think and answer

Why do you feel lighter when accelerating downward?

Show answer

The support force becomes smaller.

Slide 20

Free Fall

Gravity acts, but apparent weight is zero.

In free fall, gravity still acts on the body.

But there is no support force from a floor or bed.

So the apparent weight is zero.

This is the idea behind weightlessness.

Think and answer

In free fall, does gravity still act?

Show answer

Yes.

Slide 21

Hospital Link: Patient Lift

Tension changes when acceleration changes.

A patient lift holds a patient by a sling.

The sling tension supports the patient.

If the lift starts or stops suddenly, tension changes quickly.

That can be uncomfortable or unsafe.

Start smoothly. Stop smoothly.

Think and answer

Why must a patient lift start and stop smoothly?

Show answer

Because force/tension changes can be unsafe or uncomfortable.

Part 4 — Friction and Dynamics Tasks

First find the net force. Then use F = ma.

Slide 22

Friction

Friction opposes motion.

Friction is a force between surfaces.

It usually acts opposite to motion.

Friction can make movement harder.

But friction can also help wheels grip the floor.

Too little friction can be dangerous.

Think and answer

Does friction always act in the same direction as motion?

Show answer

No. It usually acts opposite to motion.

Slide 23

Simple Friction Idea

Surface and normal force matter.

Rough surfaces usually create more friction.

Smooth surfaces usually create less friction.

More normal force often means more friction.

A heavy loaded cart can be harder to push than an empty cart.

Think and answer

Which floor gives more friction: rough or smooth?

Show answer

Rough floor.

Slide 24

Push Cart with Friction

Find the net force first.

A person pushes a cart with 80 N.

Friction is 20 N in the opposite direction.

Net force is F_net = 80 − 20 = 60 N.

This forward net force causes acceleration.

Think and answer

What is the net force?

Show answer

60 N forward.

Slide 25

Task 1: Find Acceleration

Use a = F / m.

Cart mass is m = 30 kg.

Net force is F = 60 N.

Find acceleration.

a = F / m = 60 / 30 = 2 m/s².

Think and answer

What is the acceleration?

Show answer

2 m/s².

Slide 26

Task 2: Find Force

Use F = ma.

Hospital bed mass is m = 80 kg.

Required acceleration is a = 0.5 m/s².

Find force.

F = ma = 80 × 0.5 = 40 N.

Think and answer

What force is needed?

Show answer

40 N.

Slide 27

Task 3: Push and Friction

First find net force.

Push force is 100 N.

Friction is 30 N.

Mass is 35 kg.

F_net = 100 − 30 = 70 N.

a = 70 / 35 = 2 m/s².

Think and answer

What is the acceleration?

Show answer

2 m/s².

Slide 28

Task 4: Elevator Apparent Weight

Elevator accelerates upward.

Patient mass is 70 kg.

Elevator acceleration is 2 m/s² upward.

Use g = 10 m/s².

W_app = m(g + a).

W_app = 70 × (10 + 2) = 840 N.

Think and answer

What is the apparent weight?

Show answer

840 N.

Slide 29

Task 5: Elevator Going Down

Elevator accelerates downward.

Patient mass is 70 kg.

Elevator acceleration is 2 m/s² downward.

Use g = 10 m/s².

W_app = m(g − a).

W_app = 70 × (10 − 2) = 560 N.

Think and answer

What is the apparent weight?

Show answer

560 N.

Part 5 — Work and Energy

Work and energy connect force, motion, speed, and height.

Slide 30

Mechanical Work

Work = force × distance.

Mechanical work means a force moves an object through a distance.

If you push a cart and the cart moves, you do work.

Formula: W = F × s.

Unit: joule, J.

Think and answer

What is the formula for work?

Show answer

W = F × s.

Slide 31

Work Unit

1 joule = 1 newton × 1 meter.

The unit of work is joule.

1 J = 1 N·m.

If a 1 N force moves an object 1 m, the work is 1 J.

Work needs both force and distance.

Think and answer

What is 1 joule equal to?

Show answer

1 N·m.

Slide 32

Work Example

Push force 50 N, distance 4 m.

A person pushes a medical cart with 50 N.

The cart moves 4 m.

W = F × s.

W = 50 × 4 = 200 J.

Think and answer

How much work is done?

Show answer

200 J.

Slide 33

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy = energy of motion.

Kinetic energy is energy of motion.

Formula: E_k = 1/2 m v².

Speed is very important because it is squared.

A fast cart can be dangerous because it has more kinetic energy.

Think and answer

What happens to kinetic energy when speed increases?

Show answer

It increases strongly because speed is squared.

Slide 34

Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy = energy from height.

Potential energy is stored energy.

Gravitational potential energy depends on height.

If a patient lift raises a patient, potential energy increases.

Formula: E_p = mgh.

Think and answer

What formula do we use for gravitational potential energy?

Show answer

E_p = mgh.

Slide 35

Energy Safety Idea

More speed and more height mean more energy.

Energy is important for safety.

More speed means more kinetic energy.

More height means more potential energy.

Hospital equipment should move slowly, smoothly, and safely.

Think and answer

Why should hospital equipment avoid high speed?

Show answer

High speed means more kinetic energy and more danger.

Part 6 — More Practice and Summary

Check units. Check direction. Explain the meaning, not only the answer.

Slide 36

Task 6: Work

A cart is pushed with 40 N for 5 m.

Use W = F × s.

Force is 40 N.

Distance is 5 m.

W = 40 × 5 = 200 J.

Think and answer

How much work is done?

Show answer

200 J.

Slide 37

Task 7: Kinetic Energy

m = 20 kg, v = 2 m/s.

Use E_k = 1/2 m v².

Mass is 20 kg.

Speed is 2 m/s.

v² = 4.

E_k = 1/2 × 20 × 4 = 40 J.

Think and answer

What is the kinetic energy?

Show answer

40 J.

Slide 38

Task 8: Potential Energy

m = 70 kg, h = 1 m.

Use E_p = mgh.

Mass is 70 kg.

g = 10 m/s².

Height is 1 m.

E_p = 70 × 10 × 1 = 700 J.

Think and answer

What is the potential energy?

Show answer

700 J.

Slide 39

Mixed Task: Lift

m = 60 kg, h = 1.5 m.

A patient lift raises a patient.

Mass is 60 kg.

Height is 1.5 m.

Use g = 10 m/s².

E_p = 60 × 10 × 1.5 = 900 J.

Think and answer

What is the potential energy increase?

Show answer

900 J.

Slide 40

Common Mistakes

Check units, direction, and meaning.

kg is not force. kg is mass.

Weight is not always the same as gravitational force.

Force and energy are different ideas.

Speed and acceleration are different.

Do not forget friction and direction.

Think and answer

Is kg a unit of force?

Show answer

No. kg is a unit of mass.

Slide 41

Dynamics Summary

Force causes acceleration. Energy is connected with motion.

Today we learned: F = ma.

Gravity pulls down: F_g = mg.

Apparent weight can change.

Work is force times distance: W = F × s.

Kinetic energy is energy of motion.

Potential energy is energy of height.

Think and answer

What is the most important formula in dynamics?

Show answer

F = ma.

Slide 42

Final Message

Safe motion is slow, smooth, controlled, and predictable.

Hospital equipment is not only metal and electronics.

It moves.

When it moves, forces and energy are involved.

For patient safety, motion must be slow, smooth, controlled, and predictable.

Dynamics helps us understand why.

Think and answer

Why does dynamics matter in hospitals?

Show answer

Because forces and energy affect safety during motion.
